## Background and personal life
<div>
  <p>My name is Max Michael Andersson, I am a Swede born on the vast plains on the outskirts of Kristianstad, Skåne, who grew up by the gorgeous archipelago of Karlshamn, Blekinge, splitting my Swedish/Scanian identity.
  I have been interested in the natural and technological sciences for as long as I can remember with my microscope and binoculars as a kid trying to explore the wild world around me. Later in life, I have also become interested in the sciences on the other side of the spectrum, with more social and political identities, but I do not see myself straying from my beginnings very far, merely seeing it as an interest.
    When I am not conducting experiments, sitting in meetings as the PhD representative at the Faculty level on Campus or coding this very website, I enjoy biking, hiking and reading, as well as my recent uptake of boxing as a form of workout. I can highly recommend it! For any bike nerds out there, I built my fully custom Genesis Vagabond with full SRAM Apex Eagle 12-speed, and I love taking it on day rides, multiday rides and overnighters along the roads of Skåne.

If you look at my CV, you might spot that my academic path is all but orthodox, and you would be correct in your observation. I am not a biologist or chemist, even though I work with chemical ecology. I started a bachelor's in biology a long time ago, but felt that I was not mature enough yet to leave home to study, and so aborted my studies, a defeat at that moment, but in hindsight, the best and most mature decision I could have made. I then found my way to a bachelors in food and meal science or gastronomy, where I developed a big interest in sensory science as well as green primary production. When I then found the agroecology master's programme at SLU Alnarp, it was obvious that this was my next step in life. For my thesis, I saw myself working on IPM and olfactory responses in horticultural pests, spending days staring into a microscope like I have done so many years ago in my youth and realising when I started my PhD in biology that I have come full circle with my education and in the end I have ended up exactly where I wanted and feel like I belong.

I am a firm believer in that we are malleable enough to learn new skills and take in new information as long as we are willing as people and I think that in some ways I am a living example of this. So if you are a potential student unsure of your path forwards, listen to that gut feeling and if you are willing to understand, take in information and other perspectives than you are used to you can study and do anything you want.
  </p>
</div>
